2. For each of the following English language queries, write it down
in Regular Property Graph Logic. You do not have to evaluate the
queries on the graph G in Figure 1.

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{(a)} Return pairs of clients, c1 and c2 where client c1 transferred more
    than 5k to client c2. [2 marks]

    \item \textbf{(b)} Return pairs of clients in which client 1 initiates a transaction t
    to client 2 and client 2 initiates two transactions t1 and t2, both
    to client 1, where the total amount of t1 and t2 is the same as the
    amount of t, and t1 and t2 where initiated on different dates.
    [4 marks]

    \item \textbf{(c)} A fraud ring of length l has n clients where for i = 1, . . . , l - 1,
    client i initiates a transaction to client i + 1 with an amount of
    at least 50k, and client l initiates a transaction to client 1 with
    an amount of at least 50k.
    Return client 1 in any fraud ring of size l where l is an odd integer
    larger than 1. [4 marks]
\end{itemize}
